English I Final Project

Personal Presentation and Grammar Project

Project Objective

The objective of this project is for students to demonstrate their understanding of the basic grammar structures studied in English I through a collaborative written project and a group presentation.

Students will apply the following topics studied during the course:

  • Personal Presentation
  • Personal Pronouns
  • Verb To Be
  • Simple Present
  • Present Continuous
  • Possessive Adjectives
  • Reading Comprehension

Project Organization

Group Work

Students will work in groups of three or four members.

Written Report

The written project must contain between eight and ten pages.

Oral Presentation

The presentation will last between ten and twelve minutes.

Chapter I – Personal Presentation

Each student must prepare a personal presentation written in English. The presentation must include basic personal information and should contain between ten and twelve sentences.

The personal presentation must include the following information:

  • Full name
  • Age
  • Nationality
  • Place of residence
  • Studies or occupation
  • Languages you speak or are learning
  • Hobbies or interests
  • Favorite music or hobbies
  • Things you want to learn
  • Personal goals

Chapter II – Grammar Structures

Personal Pronouns

Students must write sentences using the following pronouns.

  • I
  • You
  • He
  • She
  • It
  • We
  • They
Verb To Be
  • Ten affirmative sentences
  • Ten negative sentences
  • Ten questions with short answers
Simple Present

Students must write ten sentences in the simple present tense describing daily routines or regular activities.

Present Continuous

Students must write ten sentences in the present continuous tense describing actions happening at the present moment.

Possessive Adjectives

Students must write seven sentences using possessive adjectives. Each sentence must use one of the following adjectives.

  • My
  • Your
  • His
  • Her
  • Its
  • Our
  • Their

Chapter III – Reading Comprehension

Reading Text

David is a university student. He is 20 years old and he lives in Santo Domingo with his parents and his younger sister.

Every day he wakes up at 6:30 in the morning. In the morning, David takes a shower, eats breakfast with his family, and prepares for his classes.

After breakfast, he goes to the university by bus. His classes start at 8:00 AM. At the university, David studies English, mathematics, and computer science.

During class, he listens to the teacher, takes notes, and participates in class activities.

At noon, David eats lunch with his friends in the university cafeteria. In the afternoon, he studies in the library and works on his assignments.

In the evening, David returns home and spends time with his family. Before going to bed, he prepares his materials for the next day.

Comprehension Questions
  1. Who is David?
  2. How old is David?
  3. Where does David live?
  4. What time does David wake up?
  5. How does David go to the university?
  6. What subjects does David study?
  7. What does David do during class?
  8. Where does David eat lunch?
  9. What does David do in the afternoon?
  10. What does David do before going to bed?

Students must answer the questions using complete sentences.

Group Presentation Roles

Students will work in groups of three or four members. Each student must participate in the oral presentation and explain a specific part of the project.

Roles for Groups of Four Students
  • All members of the group must briefly introduce themselves before the explanation of the project begins. Each student should present basic personal information as required in Chapter I (Personal Presentation).
  • Student 1 – Introduction to the project.
  • Student 2 – Explanation of Personal Pronouns and Verb To Be.
  • Student 3 – Explanation of Simple Present and Present Continuous.
  • Student 4 – Explanation of Possessive Adjectives, Reading Activity, and Conclusion.
Roles for Groups of Three Students
  • All members of the group must briefly introduce themselves before the explanation of the project begins. Each student should present basic personal information as required in Chapter I (Personal Presentation).
  • Student 1 – Introduction and Personal Presentation.
  • Student 2 – Explanation of Personal Pronouns, Verb To Be, and Possessive Adjectives.
  • Student 3 – Explanation of Simple Present, Present Continuous, Reading Activity, and Conclusion.

Conclusion

Students must write a short conclusion reflecting on their learning experience during the English I course.

The conclusion should include a personal reflection on the progress made and the goals achieved while studying English during this course.

Evaluation Criteria

Criteria Points
Grammar accuracy 25
Sentence construction 20
Personal presentation 15
Reading comprehension 15
Oral participation 15
Organization and teamwork 10
